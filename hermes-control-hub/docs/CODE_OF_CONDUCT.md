# How this repo works

I maintain Control Hub solo. This is not a corporate handbook—it is how I want collaboration to feel. I welcome all feedback, please reach out to me any any time.

## The short version

**Bring ideas. Ship results. Argue about the code, not the person.**

You can swear. You can be blunt. You can disagree loudly—as long as you are talking about the work. I would rather see a heated technical debate that ends in a merged PR than a polite thread that goes nowhere.

## What I care about

- **Good faith** — you are trying to improve the project, not waste anyone's time.
- **Honesty** — say what you mean; do not hide blockers behind vague politeness.
- **Results** — working code, clear docs, reproducible bugs, tests that prove the fix.

## What I do not care about

- Whether you used an emoji or said "please" twelve times.
- Performative niceness while blocking progress.
- Tone-policing someone who is frustrated but still on-topic.

## Hard lines

These are not "professionalism" rules—they are **do not be a menace** rules:

- **No harassment or stalking** — do not target someone across channels or keep going after they asked you to stop.
- **No threats or intimidation** — jokes included if a reasonable person would not feel safe.
- **No doxxing** — do not publish private contact info, home addresses, or secrets that are not yours to share.
- **No sexual harassment** — unwelcome sexual attention or content directed at contributors.
- **No bigotry** — attacks on people for who they are will not be tolerated, simple as that. Critique ideas and code all day long in whatever language you chose, just not the person.


## Scope

Applies in the repo (issues, PRs, discussions) and when you are clearly representing the project in public (official accounts, events, etc.).

**Report serious conduct issues** to me privately (see [.github/CODEOWNERS](../.github/CODEOWNERS))—DM or whatever confidential channel you already use.
